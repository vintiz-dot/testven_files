Key Research Takeaways

  • "Sit-and-get" PD hours have a negative correlation with tech integration.
  • Teachers suffer from a "digital competence gap" in pedagogical design.
  • A BDA (Before-During-After) coaching cycle creates measurable behavioral change.

The Pilot in Action

Our two-week trial proved that targeted coaching builds immediate capacity.

Implementation Artifacts Produced:

  • GoGuardian Scenes: Math and Science teachers built and launched custom focus scenes.
  • Device Contracts: Co-authored three-norm digital citizenship contracts with students.
  • Attention Checks: Redesigned lessons integrating Pear Deck formative assessments.
  • Walkthrough Rubric: A standardized measure for tech integration.

Focus Group Response

"What Worked"

  • Pre-work (Loom videos) saved time and reduced cognitive load during the session.
  • Sandbox environments allowed risk-free practice of GoGuardian features.
  • Teachers felt supported as designers, rather than being "evaluated".

Feedback & Iteration

  • Challenge: Time constraints inside the 90-minute blocks.
  • Solution: Shifting more foundational tech-skills to asynchronous video libraries.
  • Implication: 1:1 in-class coaching cycles remain the highest leverage activity.
