Context & Data Foundation

Why this proposal, why now
Victoria Prep went 1:1 with Chromebooks in 2024. Two years in, administration and teachers agree the devices are present — but not always purposeful. The Module 41 needs-analysis survey (n = 18 teachers + 4 administrators, conditional Google Form) exposed a predictable but painful gap between access and instructional impact.

Headline data driving this proposal (n = 18 teachers, 4 admins):
• 78% of teachers (14/18) named "students getting distracted" as their top technology challenge.
• 44% (8/18) reported they had received no training on GoGuardian, Google Classroom tab management, or other focus-keeping tools.
• 67% (12/18) rated themselves 3 or below (out of 5) on "confidence integrating technology for differentiation."
• 61% (11/18) named "classroom management with devices" as their #1 PD request — the clearest mandate in the dataset.
• 94% of teachers said Yes or Maybe to 1:1 coaching (only 1/18 declined) — the school is ready for an instructional coach.
• Admin priorities: (1) data-driven instruction, (2) purposeful device use, (3) classroom-management-with-tech look-fors for walkthroughs.
• Two teachers flagged by admin for targeted coaching: one in 7th-grade Math (data use) and one in 6th-grade Science (engagement + interactive lessons).

The four PD sessions that follow are sequenced to hit the biggest survey pain first (distractions), then ladder up to the more ambitious asks (differentiation, interactivity, data). The two coaching recommendations translate admin priorities into multi-touch, on-the-ground support.

The session runs on a Thursday PD afternoon in the 7th-grade Science classroom. Teachers bring one upcoming lesson they already know is a little flat. I open with three 60-second "tech audits" of real Victoria lessons — what the technology adds, replaces, or wastes — so the diagnosis is grounded in our building.
