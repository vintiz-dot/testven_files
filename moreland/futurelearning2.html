Reflections on the Makerspace

As documented in these recordings and the main video, the makerspace environment shifts the focus from standardized testing to authentic assessment. Teachers noted that "whatever difficulty, whatever friction, whatever initial obstacles they encounter become part of the learning process." Students expressed pride in their functional creations and learned that failure is a necessary stepping stone to success.

Lesson Artifacts: Standards, Objectives & Differentiation

Standards and Objectives

Standard Alignment (NGSS): 3-PS2-1. Plan and conduct an investigation to provide evidence of the effects of balanced and unbalanced forces on the motion of an object.

Lesson Objective: Students will construct a functional elevator model to demonstrate their understanding of electric motors, gravity, and the balancing of forces using push/pull mechanics and a counterweight.

Differentiation Strategies

To ensure accessibility for all learners, several differentiation strategies were implemented during the makerspace activity:

  • Vocabulary Scaffolding: Key terms were pre-taught in context. Instead of enforcing rigid rules, students were introduced to components and allowed to discover their functions naturally.
  • Motor Skill Support: The lesson provided physical scaffolding and instructor assistance for students with developing or limited fine motor skills.
  • Managing Anxiety: For students with a phobia of electricity, the instructor pre-taught the safety of low-voltage DC circuits to put them at ease before they interacted with the electric motor.
  • Instructor Preparation: The instructor pre-built the model to anticipate frustration points, ensuring they could offer targeted, valuable feedback exactly when students needed it.

Research & References

Makerspace integration pushes against the traditional curriculum mindset. As educational technology expert John Spencer notes, "Our world wants go-getters. It wants designers, creators, and dreamers." Makerspaces provide the physical and mental room for this to happen. By allowing students to iterate and build, learning becomes experiential, deeply rooted, and long-lasting.
