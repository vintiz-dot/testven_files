Analytic Rubric: Mathematical Modeling in Practice

Assessing: Factoring Linear Expressions in Context (7.EE.A.1, 7.EE.A.2)

Criterion Beginning (1) Developing (2) Proficient (3) Outstanding (4)
1. Mathematical Accuracy & Depth Major errors in factoring; expressions or GCF incorrect. Minor errors in factoring; partially correct GCF; limited explanation. Correct factorization of most expressions; GCF identified; explanation understandable. All expressions factored correctly; GCF and distributive property applied precisely; shows alternate factorizations or checks work.
2. Representation & Real-World Context Scenario unclear or unrelated; quantities missing; no interpretation of factors. Scenario present but missing some quantities; interpretation of factors limited. Scenario clearly described with relevant quantities; factored form interpreted (e.g., number of kits, mural dimensions). Scenario richly described; quantities and units labelled; factored form used to make informed decisions and connections to studentsâ€™ lives.
3. Explanation & Academic Language Little or no use of vocabulary; explanation difficult to follow. Some vocabulary used; explanation partially clear; incomplete sentences. Uses key terms (factor, GCF, distributive) accurately; explains reasoning in complete sentences. Precise academic language throughout; explains reasoning logically; may draw on bilingual terms when helpful; includes clear justifications.
4. Critical Thinking & Problem Solving No evidence of evaluation; chooses arbitrary groupings or dimensions. Attempts to justify grouping but lacks analysis of efficiency; limited reasoning. Evaluates different grouping/dimension options; justifies choice using math. Compares multiple strategies; selects most efficient or creative solution; discusses trade-offs and reflects on reasoning.
5. Communication & Collaboration Work disorganized; roles unclear; minimal participation; difficulties in presenting. Some organization; uneven participation; presentation lacks clarity. Organized work; all members contribute; presentation understandable. Highly organized; equitable participation; presentation engaging and clear; group responds to questions confidently.
