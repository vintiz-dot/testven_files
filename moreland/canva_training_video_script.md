## Production notes

- **Title cards:** Use Canva's *YouTube Intro* templates for the open/close. Export each 2s card as PNG or MP4 so they can be dropped directly into the timeline.
- **Music:** Pixabay (CC-BY) loop — suggested: "Inspiring Corporate" or "Upbeat Ukulele." Keep bed at **-18 LUFS** under voice, **-10 LUFS** on transitions.
- **B-roll:** Capture three passes of classroom footage (teacher at board, student on laptop, close-up of hand scrolling Canva) OR use Canva's free *Education* video stock.
- **Lower thirds:** Canva template *"Presenter Lower Third — Purple"* with your name and role.
- **Captions:** Use Canva's auto-caption on the final render, then hand-correct. Required for accessibility.
- **Outro CTA:** Direct viewers to the portfolio page (this file's sibling `canva-training.html`) and the embedded survey.

**VISUAL**

**NARRATION**
> Here's your first step. Go to **canva.com/education** and click *"Get Verified."* You'll upload your teaching credential — district ID, state license, or school email all work — and within about 48 hours your account unlocks everything premium teachers pay for: *all* templates, *all* stock photos, AI image generation, magic resize, and the thing we're going to care about most today: **Classrooms**.
>
> Once you're in, create a classroom. I'm calling mine "Grade 7 Science." Click *Invite Students* — Canva gives you a class code or a link, drop it in Google Classroom or Canvas or Schoology, and your students are in. No email setup, no permission forms.

### 01:45 – 03:30 · Core features demo

**VISUAL**
- Screencast walk-through. Keep pace fast — 10–15 seconds per feature. Use *zoom-in* effect for small UI elements.

#### 1:45 – 2:05 — Templates
Search bar: type **"7th grade science."** Sort by "Most relevant." Hover over three templates to show live preview.

**NARRATION**
> Feature one: templates. Search by grade, subject, or keyword. *"7th grade science"* — 240 results. Infographics, slide decks, worksheets, posters. You never start from a blank canvas.

#### 2:05 – 2:35 — Magic Resize
Open any template. Click *Resize & Magic Switch*. Convert a slide deck to an Instagram story to a printable worksheet.

**NARRATION**
> Feature two: Magic Resize. One design — click once — and you have the same content formatted as a slide deck, a printable handout, and even a social post for parents. This is the killer feature. It's in the *Resize* button at the top right.

#### 2:35 – 3:00 — Brand kit
Go to *Brand Hub*. Add school colors, logo, typography. Apply to current design in one click.

**NARRATION**
> Feature three: your Brand Kit. Upload your school colors, your logo, and the two fonts you use all year. Every new design snaps to those automatically — which means *everything* you produce looks like it came from the same classroom.

#### 3:00 – 3:30 — Collaboration & comments
Share a design link. Show simulated real-time cursors (use Canva's demo or record yourself collaborating with a second browser window).

**NARRATION**
> And feature four: collaboration. Share a design the same way you share a Google Doc. Students — or co-teachers — edit in real time. You can leave comments right on the design, and Canva tracks every version.

**ON-SCREEN TEXT**
- Feature chyrons (one at a time): *"Templates"* · *"Magic Resize"* · *"Brand Kit"* · *"Collaboration"*

### 03:30 – 04:30 · Student view & assignments

**VISUAL**
- Split-screen animation: teacher's assignment panel on the left, student's Canva view on the right.
- Show the *"Assign to class"* button.
- Cut to a dummy student view, showing a template the teacher pushed out.
- Show the teacher's feedback comment popping up on the student's design.

**NARRATION**
> Let's flip perspectives. Here's what your student sees. They log into Canva with the class code. On their dashboard they see one thing: the assignment you just pushed out. They open it, edit on the template, and submit without ever leaving Canva. You review it, leave a comment, maybe mark a specific object on the design, and they see your feedback the next time they log in. This is the workflow — *assign, create, comment, revise* — that makes Canva feel less like a design app and more like a learning environment.

**ON-SCREEN TEXT**
- Lower third: *"Teacher view"* ↔ *"Student view"*
- Callout: *"Feedback lives on the design itself"*

### 04:30 – 06:15 · Five classroom examples across grade bands

**VISUAL**
Rapid-cut montage. Each example = ~20 seconds. Use a consistent lower third: *"Example N · Grade Band · Subject."* Show one mocked-up Canva screen per example.

#### 04:30 – 04:50 — Example 1: 7th–8th Math, Visual Proof One-Pagers
Screen: template with a triangle, labeled sides, step-by-step reasoning box.

**NARRATION**
> Example one. 7th and 8th grade math. Students pick a theorem — Pythagorean, triangle inequality — and build a one-page visual proof. Forces them to *explain* the math, not just solve it.

#### 04:50 – 05:10 — Example 2: 6th–7th Science, Lab Safety + Animated Cell
Screen: cell diagram with draggable organelles; below it, a lab safety infographic.

**NARRATION**
> Example two. Middle school science. A lab safety infographic remixed in pairs — and then an animated cell diagram with an audio callout on each organelle. Structure-and-function, but spatial.

#### 05:10 – 05:30 — Example 3: 9th–10th ELA, Character-study book covers
Screen: two student covers side by side, different characters, same novel.

**NARRATION**
> Example three. 9th and 10th grade ELA. Students design a novel's book cover through one character's perspective, with a 150-word artist statement on the back. Every design choice becomes evidence-based textual analysis.

#### 05:30 – 05:50 — Example 4: 3rd–4th Grade, All-About-Me + Class Newsletter
Screen: grid of colorful student cards, then a newsletter template.

**NARRATION**
> Example four. Elementary. Every student builds an "All About Me" card week one, and you fold them into a class newsletter that goes home to families. Student voice *and* parent engagement, one tool.

#### 05:50 – 06:15 — Example 5: 7th Grade Social Studies, Annotated Timeline
Screen: long-scroll timeline with era cards color-coded by theme.

**NARRATION**
> And example five. 7th grade social studies. A long-scroll timeline of a civilization or movement. Each era gets a primary source, a 30-word caption, and a color-coded tag: political, economic, cultural. Magic Resize turns it into a printable poster and a shared digital deck.

## How to swap the placeholder with your real video

1. Open `canva-training.html` in an editor.
2. Find the `<div class="video-placeholder-content">` block inside `<div class="video-frame" id="videoFrame">`.
3. Replace it with one of:

```html
<!-- YouTube / Vimeo embed -->
<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/YOUR_VIDEO_ID"
        title="Canva for Teachers PD"
        allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope"
        allowfullscreen></iframe>
```

```html
<!-- Direct .mp4 -->
<video src="canva-training.mp4" controls preload="metadata"
       poster="canva-training-poster.jpg"></video>
```

4. Save. The page needs no rebuild — refresh the portfolio tab in `masters.html` and the new video plays inline.
