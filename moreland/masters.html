Hi, I'm Victor Moronu. I'm a Middle School Mathematics and Science Educator, MDE Specialist, and former Mechanical Engineer utilizing technology to bridge the gap between abstract concepts and accessible student experiences.

My "Why"

I entered the EdTech field to advocate for tools built with the classroom in mind. I believe that educational technology should not merely direct student learning, but act as an assistive key that inspires student autonomy and agency.

Too often, differently-abled students are treated as "edge cases" in software development. My mission is to ensure that student wellness and profound inclusivity are rooted directly in the business logic of educational programs. When deployed correctly, technology isn't a barrier—it's the ultimate tool for universal access.

Activity 1: My "Why" and EdTech Goals

The Engineer-Educator

Blending a background in Mechanical Engineering (B.Eng) with modern pedagogical expertise, I operate on the non-negotiable principle that child safety and emotional wellbeing are the absolute prerequisites for academic success.

My teaching philosophy is rooted in the belief that education is not preparation for life; education is life itself. In my classroom, this translates to Failing Forward (creating a psychologically safe space for prototyping and iterating) and providing an Authentic Context (using math and physics as tools to understand the real world).

Having successfully completed the rigorous TEACH-NOW Teacher Preparation Program, I have now transitioned into the Master's in Educational Technology track to develop district-wide technology improvement plans and bridge the gap between software engineers and authentic classroom experiences.

Gibbs' Reflective Cycle

1. Description (What happened?)

I am actively working towards earning external EdTech certifications, specifically targeting Google Certified Educator and Apple Teacher credentials.

2. Feelings (What were you thinking and feeling?)

I feel motivated to formally validate my digital skills, though preparing for standardized certification exams alongside my master's coursework can feel slightly demanding.

3. Evaluation (What was good and bad about the experience?)

The good aspect is that the training modules expose me to features I haven’t fully utilized. The challenge is finding the dedicated time to complete the performance-based assessments.

4. Analysis (What sense can you make of the situation?)

Earning these certifications is critical for my role as an EdTech leader. It provides me with a standardized, recognized foundation that builds trust when I am coaching or consulting with peers.

5. Conclusion (What else could you have done?)

I could map the certification study paths directly to the specific problems of practice I am investigating in my Master's coursework to create synergy.

6. Action Plan (If it arose again, what would you do?)

Moving forward, I will schedule dedicated 'certification sprints' on weekends and immediately test new tools learned in the classroom to reinforce my understanding before exams.

Gibbs' Reflective Cycle

1. Description (What happened?)

I collaborated with my cohort to research, vet, and curate a comprehensive database of 8-10 EdTech applications categorized by their pedagogical use case (Assessment, Management, etc.).

2. Feelings (What were you thinking and feeling?)

I felt a sense of clarity working collaboratively. Pooling our collective classroom experiences made it much easier to separate flashy tools from genuinely effective ones.

3. Evaluation (What was good and bad about the experience?)

The peer collaboration was excellent and provided diverse perspectives. However, keeping the list concise was difficult as there are so many tools on the market.

4. Analysis (What sense can you make of the situation?)

As Michael Fullan says, 'Pedagogy is the driver.' Developing this resource highlighted that the value of an EdTech tool lies entirely in its specific instructional deployment, not in its novelty.

5. Conclusion (What else could you have done?)

While we categorized by use case, the toolkit could be improved by including a 'barrier to entry' rating for teachers who are less tech-savvy.

6. Action Plan (If it arose again, what would you do?)

I will use this curated presentation as a living document. During instructional coaching sessions, I will use this toolkit as a rapid-response database when a teacher presents a specific student learning barrier.
