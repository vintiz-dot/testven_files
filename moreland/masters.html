Hi, I'm Victor Moronu. I'm a Middle School Mathematics and Science Educator, MDE Specialist, and former Mechanical Engineer utilizing technology to bridge the gap between abstract concepts and accessible student experiences.

My "Why"

I entered the EdTech field to advocate for tools built with the classroom in mind. I believe that educational technology should not merely direct student learning, but act as an assistive key that inspires student autonomy and agency.

Too often, differently-abled students are treated as "edge cases" in software development. My mission is to ensure that student wellness and profound inclusivity are rooted directly in the business logic of educational programs. When deployed correctly, technology isn't a barrier—it's the ultimate tool for universal access.

Activity 1: My "Why" and EdTech Goals

The Engineer-Educator

Blending a background in Mechanical Engineering (B.Eng) with modern pedagogical expertise, I operate on the non-negotiable principle that child safety and emotional wellbeing are the absolute prerequisites for academic success.

My teaching philosophy is rooted in the belief that education is not preparation for life; education is life itself. In my classroom, this translates to Failing Forward (creating a psychologically safe space for prototyping and iterating) and providing an Authentic Context (using math and physics as tools to understand the real world).

Having successfully completed the rigorous TEACH-NOW Teacher Preparation Program, I have now transitioned into the Master's in Educational Technology track to develop district-wide technology improvement plans and bridge the gap between software engineers and authentic classroom experiences.
